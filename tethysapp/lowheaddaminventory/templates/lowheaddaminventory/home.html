Project Proposal - Evacuation Least Cost Path App

Introduction

The Utah Lowhead Dam Inventory App was made to create an updated inventory of lowhead dams in Utah. Lowhead dams are mostly inconspicuous dams that have a very small dropoff. The small dropoff creates a strong current which is called a backwash. Backwash can pose great danger to anyone who gets caught in it. The circular current will drag people under the water, possibly for many minutes, which can lead the person to be drowned. Below is a picture of a lowhead dam showing the area of backwashat lowhead dams.

Why make an inventory?

Since lowhead dams are quite inconspicuous few states have an updated inventory of lowhead dams. Although professionals in water resources may have knowledge of where dams are, other agencies, like like water recreation offices, could use a lowhead dam inventory to take precautionary steps to prevent future accidents from occuring at lowhead dams.

How can you Help?

Anyone with knowledge of a location of a lowhead dam is instrumental in creating an accurate inventory. If you have knowledge of a lowhead dam you can use the app to add a location and description of the lowhead dam you know of. If you have knowledge of where a lowhead dam exists proceed to the "Add a Lowhead Dam" button found to the left.

Existing Dams

The "Existing Lowhead Dams" button found to the left will lead you to a list of existing lowhead dams, and their description.
